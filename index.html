  • While there is no specific timeline when Blackjack started, researchers have reasons to believe that it began in French casinos around the 18th century. Blackjack has few other names, but the French called it Vingt-et-Un, translated it means twenty-one. Blackjack was also commonly called twenty-one by the English.

  • Others believe the game first originated during the Roman era, for which wooden blocks were utilised to denote the numbers involved, however this is not confirmed.

  • It's to be said the way Blackjack got its name, is casinos offered bonus payouts to stimulate players' interest. The bonus was a ten-to-one payout if player's were dealt the Ace of Spades and a black jack. This hand was called "Blackjack". The name stuck with it even after they got rid of the bonuses.

Terminology

Here is a list of some very common terms that are used.
It is important to know these terms when playing Blackjack.

  • Blackjack - a two card hand consisting of an ace and any 10 value card.
  • Box - the area in front of each player where bets are placed.
  • Bust - to go over a hand total of 21.
  • Hit - to take another card.
  • Hard Hand - a hand that does not contain an ace, meaning it can only have one total value.
  • Hole Card - the dealer's face down card.
  • Pair - a two card hand containing equal value cards. Pairs may be split.
  • Soft Hand - a hand containing an ace valued as a 1, meaning another card may be taken.
  • Stand - to not take any further cards - also known as "sitting".

Rules and How To Play

  1. Each player, including the dealer, starts with two cards.
  2. The Dealer has one hidden card that will be shown at the end of the round.
  3. The goal is to beat the dealer without exceeding 21.
  4. If player exceeds 21, they bust and the dealer automatically wins.
  5. Each card is worth its normal value except Jacks, Queens
    and Kings are worth 10, and Aces can be used as a 1 or 11.
  6. If the dealer busts and the player does not, the player wins.
  7. All similar score less than 21 result in a draw.
  8. At each turn the player can hit (ask for a card),
    stand (remain with it's current score) or,
    double down (the bet is doubled but only one more card is drawn.)
  9. If player has two identical cards, they can split, which means
    they can make two separate hands that will be played independently.
